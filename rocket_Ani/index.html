Liquid-Propellant Rockets

Use separate liquid fuel & oxidizer, offering throttling & restart (e.g., SpaceX Falcon 9, Saturn V).

Solid-Propellant Rockets

Fuel & oxidizer mixed solid; simple, reliable for boosters & fireworks, but can't be stopped easily.

Monopropellant Rockets

Use a single propellant (like hydrazine) decomposed by a catalyst, common in small spacecraft thrusters.

Electric Propulsion

Ion thrusters & Hall thrusters use electric/magnetic fields for high efficiency over long durations (deep space probes).

Nuclear Thermal Rockets

Use a nuclear reactor to heat propellant, offering high performance (experimental).

Hybrid Rockets

Solid fuel with liquid oxidizer, balancing simplicity & control.
